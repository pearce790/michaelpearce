About Me

I am a fifth-year PhD candidate in statistics at the University of Washington. My primary research interests include preference learning, peer review, and Bayesian models applied to the social sciences. I currently work with Elena Erosheva on unified modeling of scores and rankings in peer review and other social science applications. Previously, I worked with Adrian Raftery on probabilistic forecasting of supercentenarian survival using Bayesian population projections. In addition to research, I am actively involved in the statistics teaching community at UW. In addition to serving as a teaching assistant, I am a co-founder and organizer of a reading group in statistics education, 7-time undergraduate Directed Reading Program mentor, and served on a committee to update and modernize the department's undergraduate curriculum. I have spent two summers as an Applied Statistics intern with the Applied Math research group at Boeing Research and Technology. Prior to grad school I consulted for Deloitte in data analytics, specifically working with banking and financial services clients.

In my free time, I enjoy running and biking along Puget Sound's beautiful waterways and trails, bouldering, baking cookies or cakes, going to the many breweries in my neighborhood, and reading memoirs. In 2020, I completed a quest to visit every one of Seattle's 413 city parks by my own power - either running or biking! You can visualize all the parks I visited here.
