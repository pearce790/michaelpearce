About Me

I am a third-year PhD student in statistics at the University of Washington. My primary research interests include nonparametric theory and Bayesian models applied to the social sciences. I currently work with Elena Erosheva on simultaneous modeling of scores and rankings from academic panel review processes, as well as with Adrian Raftery on probabilistic forecasting of supercentenarian survival using Bayesian population projections. I have spent two summers as an Applied Statistics intern with the Applied Math research group at Boeing Research and Technology. Prior to grad school I consulted for Deloitte in data analytics.

In my free time, I enjoy running and biking along Puget Sound's beautiful waterways and trails, going to the many breweries in my local Ballard neighborhood, and reading memoirs. I also recently completed a quest to visit every one of Seattle's 413 city parks by my own power - either running or biking! You can visualize all the parks I visited here.
