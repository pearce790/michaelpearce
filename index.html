About Me

I am a second-year PhD student in statistics at the University of Washington. My primary research interests include nonparametric clustering methods, especially in high-dimensional data. I currently work with Werner Stuetzle on the development and analysis of generalized single-linkage clustering, as well as with Adrian Raftery on probabilistic forecasting of supercentenarian survival using Bayesian population projections. In addition, I am an Applied Statistics Intern at Boeing Research and Technology (currently on educational leave).

In my free time, I enjoy running along Seattle's beautiful waterways and trails, skiing, and cooking.
