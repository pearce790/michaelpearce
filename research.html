Probabilistic forecasting of maximum human lifespan to 2100 using Bayesian population projections
In this project with Professor Adrian E. Raftery, we quantified the human lifespan using a statistical approach that probabilistically forecasts the maximum reported age at death (MRAD) through 2100. Using the existing exponential survival model for supercentenarians (people over age 110), we quantify population uncertainty using Bayesian population projections and incorporate the most recent data from the International Database on Longevity to obtain unconditional estimates of the distribution of MRAD this century in a fully Bayesian analysis. This work was published in June 2021 in the journal Demographic Research.

stoBEST: An efficient methodology for increased spatial resolution in two-component molecular tagging velocimetry
This NSF-funded project was completed as a senior undergraduate student at St. Olaf College as part of the Center for Interdisciplinary Research. We developed a new methodology to read and analyze pairs of molecular tagging velocimetry images, a technique for studying high-speed gaseous flows in a chemical experiment. The proposed method de-noises and processes the original images and then uses a variety of nonparametric techniques to create high-resolution two-component velocity fields. Our results are compared against numerous existing alternatives and shown to improve accuracy and resolution in a variety of cases. This work was published in 2020 in the journal Measurement Science and Technology.
